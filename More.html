what makes our coffee special?

the coffee quality is beyond the final product served on your cup. Its quality springs from the start—the type of coffee beans grown, the region, roasting, and brewing, down to the actual coffee served right under your nose. That said, here's why speciality coffee makes coffee all the more special...

Global Ethical Conciousness

What’s good about today’s coffee industry is that it is leaning more towards global ethic consciousness. The market understands that coffee is a culture that resonates well with people. As a thriving coffee lover community, its constant production and proliferation continue. Farmers, suppliers, entrepreneurs, and even baristas are all given opportunities. In the past, coffee had a bleak history, given the farming oppression, manipulated poverty, and violent occupation in the industry. Today, the coffee community strives to provide many opportunities for people to make a fair living.

The Quality of Coffee

There’s more to speciality coffee than the looks of it. If there’s one great thing about it, that is its quality. If you visit a coffee shop and see a good, experienced barista do their job, you’ll be amazed. You’ll notice the barista weigh the coffee beans on a scale, grind them, and pour them with a unique technique. Seeing how a barista does the preparation is one thing; having the actual aroma and taste of the speciality coffee is another. You’ll savour something like no other with it's rich flavour notes and strong aroma, no matter what your order is. This is because the industry pays particular attention to the quality of the coffee beans, the use of proper tools and machines, and the right preparation techniques.

The SOURCING, ROASTING, and BREWING

As mentioned above, speciality coffee is quality driven. Unlike any other coffee movement in the history of the world, the quality of speciality coffee can be attributed to three main factors, as follows: Sourcing: The speciality coffee industry includes vendors and roasters who are participating in Direct Trade sourcing. By going to the farms themselves and making deals directly, they can choose the best coffees from farms that provide high grade coffee beans and fit in with their ethical standards. Roasting: The speciality coffee industry doesn’t just follow the same roasting formulas used for almost centuries. Roasters perfect their roasting techniques and profiles, adopting a different approach and sometimes scientific method in roasting coffee, depending on the type of bean. With speciality coffee, you can have subtle flavours like blueberries, milk chocolate, honey, mangoes, star anise, cane sugar, and beyond, all of which are brought out due to the finely honed techniques developed by speciality roasters. Brewing: There’s a huge difference between speciality coffee shops and shops outside this segment. This gap boils down to the coffee brewing methods. Speciality coffee uses proper tools and equipment for precise brewing, guranteeing a quality coffee experience.

FINAL WORDS

There’s more to speciality coffee than meets the eye. It takes into full consideration an approach to coffee sparked by global conscious ethics, a rich appreciation for quality and diversity, and a thriving community of coffee lovers across the globe. With the valuable benefits and experience of speciality coffee, you have all the right reasons to go for it and savour the taste and aroma it brings. The Cafe-Lorant only offers the finest speciality coffee taking into account the origins of the speciality coffee beans, ethical standards and by calling uppon the expertise of master coffee roasters.
